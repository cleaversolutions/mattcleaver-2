If you've been watching the coverage of NASCAR's Daytona Speedweeks & Daytona 500, you've probably noticed that besides Danica Patrick the hot topic of conversation has been the new-fangled electronic fuel injection (EFI) systems used on the cars.

One commentator said,

We're just not going to know how these new systems behave when a car stalls on pit road or runs out of gas.

It's a bit interesting how they speak of this technology as something so new and unknown, seeing as our $500 racecar from 1986 is equipped with said technology, which has been in development since the 1950s. I'm pretty sure the engineers will have this figured out just fine.

With every year, NASCAR is inching ever closer to embracing the technology of our $500 racecar. Of course, we still have an independent rear suspension.
